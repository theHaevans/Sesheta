Heather Evans

Emma's dad had a horrible temper when she was growing up, and he often took it out on both her and her mom. Her mom taught her how to hide the bruises and make the excuses that waived off relevant authorities. She moved away right after finishing high school to get free, but her mom has stayed with him. With the help of a therapist and good friends, Emma has learned to speak up for herself in both her personal and professional life. She works at an architecture firm.

Vicente Malave

Jack's parents welcomed a seeming friend into their family when he was in the third grade. This friend used their trust to molest Jack for years, convincing his parents that his complaints were due to an overactive imagination. After a suicide attempt, his university sent him to a counselor who saved his life. Jack is now pursuing a masters in social work so he can offer hope to others who feel hopeless.

Still I rise

By Maya Angelou

You may write me down in history
With your bitter, twisted lies,
You may trod me in the very dirt
But still, like dust, I'll rise.

Does my sassiness upset you?
Why are you beset with gloom?
'Cause I walk like I;ve got oil wells
Pumping in my living room.

Just like moons and like sons,
With the certainty of tides,
Just like hopes springing high,
Still I'll rise.

Did you want to see me broken?
Bowed head and lowered eyes?
Shoulders falling down like teardrops, Weakened by my soulful cries?

Does my haughtiness offend you?
Don't you take it awful hard
'Cause I laugh like I've got gold mines
Diggin' in my own backyard.

You may shoot me with your words,
You may cut me with your eyes,
You may kill me with your hatefulness,
But still, like air, I'll rise.

Does my sexiness upset you?
Does it come as a surprise
That I dance like I've got diamonds
At the meeting of my thighs?

Out of the huts of history's shame
I rise
Up from a past that's rooted in pain
I rise
I'm a black ocean, leaping and wide,
Welling and swelling I bear in the tide.

Leaving behind the nights of terror and fear
I rise
Into a daybreak that's wondrously clear
I rise
Bringing the gifts that my ancestors gave,
I am the dream and the hope of the slave.
I rise
I rise
I rise.
